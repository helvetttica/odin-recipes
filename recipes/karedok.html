Karedok Recipe


Karedok is a traditional Sundanese raw vegetable salad originating from West Java, Indonesia. It features thinly sliced or shredded vegetables like raw long beans, cabbage, bean sprouts, and cucumber, often mixed with tofu and tempeh. The dish is brought together by a distinctive peanut sauce, which is typically made from peanuts, chili peppers, garlic, kaempferia galanga (known as kencur in Indonesia), palm sugar, and tamarind. The ingredients are usually pounded together using a mortar and pestle, creating a vibrant and flavorful dressing that coats the fresh vegetables. Karedok is usually served with rice cakes (lontong) or plain rice and is often garnished with kerupuk (crackers) for added texture. It's a healthy, refreshing, and distinctly Indonesian dish.


ingredients:

  • Long beans
  • Bean sprouts
  • Cabbage
  • Thai basil
  • Cucumber
  • Spicy peanut sauce


Steps:

  1. Prepare the vegetables: Wash and thinly slice raw vegetables like cabbage, long beans, bean sprouts, and cucumber. Grate carrots.
  2. Make the peanut sauce: Grind chilies and garlic, then add toasted peanuts, palm sugar, and salt. Pound or blend until smooth.
  3. Add liquid to the sauce: Gradually add a little water and tamarind juice to the peanut sauce, mixing until it reaches a smooth, pourable consistency.
  4. Combine vegetables and sauce: Place all the prepared vegetables in a bowl and pour the peanut sauce over them.
  5. Toss and serve: Gently toss the vegetables with the sauce until well combined. Serve immediately, optionally garnished with fried shallots or kerupuk (crackers).
